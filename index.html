Artist Statement


With increasing barriers to the Disability Support Pension (DSP), 36% of people currently on JobSeeker are disabled or sick (i.e. 1 in 3) and only 17% are exempt from fulfilling job application quotas, also known as “mutual obligations.”

‘look!’ is a humorous critique on my experience as one of many JobSeekers who are unable to adequately demonstrate—or perhaps perform—their disability for a discriminatory welfare system. Supported by a song from the Centrelink hold line, ‘look!’ ruminates on the never-ending job hunt, the performativity of work, and the discomfort of being a trans JobSeeker.

I am bending over backwards; I am looking for work; I am watching you back.

Come watch me look!

Artist Bio


Zeth Cameron (they/them) is a Naarm-based multidisciplinary artist and designer. Their practice follows an open-ended creative process that is dictated by the logics of play and informed by their experiences with neurodivergence and gender nonconformity. Zeth is a graduate from Monash University with a Bachelor of Communication Design (2020), and recently completed their Creative Arts (Honours) at Deakin University (2021). They are an emerging curator at the Boroondara Gallery of Young Art, as well as through Melbourne's SIGNAL Young Curators program, and were previously the Graphic Designer at Trocadero Art Space.
